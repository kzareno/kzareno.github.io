Kaitlin Zareno

Hi! I'm Kait, a 3rd-year Computer Science PhD student at the University of Southern California (Fight on!). I am incredibly fortunate to be co-advised by Professor Maja Matarić in the Interaction Lab and Professor Shri Narayanan in the Signal Analysis and Interpretation Lab (SAIL). I'm interested personalized wellbeing and understanding how people recover from everyday stressors. To that end, my research centers around understanding longitudinal bio-behavioral dynamics, particularly in response to mental health interventions. I aim to build intelligent, adaptive technologies by translating complex physiological and behavioral data into actionable, personalized insights that promote long-term mental wellness.


Prior to USC, I received both my Bachelor's and MEng degrees in Computer Science from the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT) in 2023 and 2024. While at MIT, I was a member of the women's varsity soccer team and conducted research in both the Tadesse Lab and the MIT Media Lab's Personal Robots Group.
